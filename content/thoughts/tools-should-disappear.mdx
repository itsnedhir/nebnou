There's a version of using a tool where you stop noticing it. The keyboard, the editor, the language. It becomes an extension of thought rather than an obstacle between thought and output.

Most tools never reach that state. They stay visible — demanding attention, requiring workarounds, generating friction at exactly the wrong moment.

## what makes a tool disappear

Speed is part of it. Latency breaks the illusion. But more important is **predictability** — knowing exactly what a tool will do before you do it. Surprise costs attention.

The other thing is constraint. A tool with too many options forces decisions that shouldn't need to be made. A sharp constraint removes the decision entirely.

> A good tool has an opinion. A great tool makes you forget it has one.

## the ones that disappeared for me

- `vim` motions — muscle memory, no thought
- the terminal — one interface for everything
- plain text — no format to fight

The common thread: low ceremony, high control.
